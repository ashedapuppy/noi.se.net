A little rant about guitar, accessibility and the beauty of \*rock adjacent\* music

<!-- more -->

One thing I like reading about is guitarists with non traditional experiences,
not the Steve Vai's of the world, who were born with long fingers and large hands,
not the Buckethead's, abled cis and white.

I like reading about those guitarists who had to overcome adversity, whatever
that may be, in order to get their sound out there.

---

## Django my heart (and soul)

Django Reinhardt (1910-1953), At the age of 17, having just gotten married Django
suffered extensive burns over the left half of his body, leaving his leg nigh
unusable, and almost entirely depriving him of the use of his ring and pinky fingers.

This never stopped him from pursuing his passion in music, and his love of Jazz,
he would tour throughout France (eww), earning little but little by little
developing his own style, characterized by his unparalleled virtuosity,
innovative improvisation, and unique blending of jazz, Romani, and classical
influences. His soulful chord voicings and lightning-fast runs would put
modern sweep pickers to shame.

He pioneered the guitar as a solo instrument, creating a repertoire that would go
on to influence the greats of both the classical and emerging rock worlds,
getting name-dropped by the likes Tony Iomi, Jeff Beck, Jerry Garcia and
the one guitarist from the Beetles (//neg //hj).

All this with two and a half fingers on his fretting hand.
